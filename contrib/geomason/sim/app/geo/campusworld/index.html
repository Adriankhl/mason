GeoMASON: CampusWorld

By Mark Coletti and Keith Sullivan
The CampusWorld GeoMASON example shows how to import GIS data, and how to move agents when they are limited to paths defined by the GIS data.

The simulation starts by reading GIS data describing the buildings, roads, and walkways of the George Mason University Fairfax campus. All the associated attribute data from the GIS files is available through the inspectors. The simulation then ranomly places 1000 agents on the walkways, and moves them around randomly. We do this by converting the walkways into a PlanarGraph, and then move the agents along segements of the graph. When the agents reach an intersection, they randomly choose a new segment and continue on.
